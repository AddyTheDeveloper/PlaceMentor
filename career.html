    Behavioral Interview Scenarios (CO6)

    "Tell me about a time you failed."
    Focus on the learning and recovery, not the failure itself.

    ScenarioBest Approach
    Conflict in TeamExplain how you mediated using data and neutral communication.
    Tight DeadlineShowcase prioritization and time management (Agile mindset).
    Strength / WeaknessWeakness should be real but actionable (e.g., "I sometimes over-focus on details").
    Why should we hire you?Align your unique skills with the company's current challenges/needs.
    Where do you see yourself in 5 years?Focus on growth, skill mastery, and contributing to the organization's goals.

    Effective Communication (CO5)

    • Clarity: Don't use jargon unless necessary.
    • Confidence: Maintain eye contact and posture (even in virtual rounds).
    • Curiosity: Always ask 2-3 questions to the interviewer at the end.

    The "Interview Eve" Checklist

    Last 24 Hours: Don't try to learn new topics. Focus on revision and mental preparation.

    • Review the Basics: Skim through the "Placement FAQs" in each unit of this platform.
    • Research the Company: Know their products, recent news, and core values.
    • Logistics: Check your internet (if virtual) or plan your route (if physical). Keep your outfit ready.
    • Questions for Interviewer: Prepare at least 3 thoughtful questions (e.g., "What does a typical day look like for an associate engineer here?").
    • Sleep: 7-8 hours of rest is mandatory for cognitive performance.
